NBC Subsidiary v. Superior Court
June 4, 2012

Only in the rarest of circumstances may a court bar the press and public from a trial.
Sandra Locke sued Clint Eastwood under several causes of action arising out of alleged promises to assist Locke in the development of motion picture projects. The trial court excluded the public and the press from all proceedings held outside the presence of the jury, and sealed the transcripts of those proceedings, in order to prevent the jury from receiving unauthorized information about the trial. The Supreme Court held that in certain very limited circumstances may a court bar the press from the courtroom in order to guarantee a fair trial to the parties. The assertion that publicity might deprive a party of a free trial is not enough to overcome the First Amendment right of access. The Court noted that it will presume that jurors generally follow instructions to avoid media coverage and to disregard coverage that they happen to hear or see.
